Ginger Scallion White Fish Stir-Fry

Yield: 4 servings
Prep Time: 27 minutes
Cook Time: 9 minutes
Total Time: 36 minutes
Fresh ginger and bright scallions create an aromatic foundation that awakens the delicate fish, while Shaoxing wine adds depth and complexity. The crisp-tender vegetables provide textural contrast against the silky fish, with sesame oil bringing everything together in a glossy, fragrant finish that celebrates the essence of Cantonese cooking.

This elegant stir-fry exemplifies the Cantonese philosophy of honoring fresh ingredients through minimal but precise seasoning, transforming the classic ginger-scallion pairing from its traditional steamed fish application into a vibrant wok dish. Rooted in the coastal cooking traditions of Southern China, where fresh fish was abundant and aromatics like ginger and scallions were prized for their ability to enhance rather than mask natural flavors, this recipe adapts the time-honored combination for the modern home kitchen. The technique of coating fish pieces in cornstarch before stir-frying creates a delicate protective layer that keeps the fish tender while allowing it to absorb the aromatic sauce, while the generous use of fresh ginger provides both warmth and brightness that cuts through the richness. Unlike heavy, sauce-laden stir-fries, this dish maintains the Cantonese ideal of wok hei—the elusive "breath of the wok"—where high heat and quick cooking preserve the individual character of each ingredient while creating a harmonious whole.

INGREDIENTS
  • 1½ pounds white fish fillets (cod, halibut, or sea bass)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon white pepper
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (light soy preferred)
  • 3 tablespoons Shaoxing rice wine or dry sherry
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 3 inches fresh ginger
  • 6 scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 4 ounces snow peas
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil (divided)
EQUIPMENT
  • Wok or large (12-inch) skillet
  • Sharp knife for prep work
  • Cutting board
  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Small bowl for sauce
  • Wooden spoon or wok spatula
  • Serving plate
PREPARATION
Prepare Ingredients (15 min)
Cut 1½ pounds fish into 1-inch cubes and pat completely dry with paper towels. Peel 3 inches fresh ginger and julienne into thin strips. Cut 6 scallions into 2-inch pieces, separating whites and greens. Mince 2 cloves garlic. Slice 1 red bell pepper into strips. Trim 4 ounces snow peas by snapping off the stem ends and removing any strings.
Season Fish (10 min)
In a medium bowl, toss fish cubes with 2 tablespoons cornstarch, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon white pepper until evenly coated. Let sit for 10 minutes to allow the coating to adhere—this creates a protective layer that keeps the fish tender during stir-frying.
Prepare Sauce (2 min)
In a small bowl, whisk together 3 tablespoons soy sauce, 3 tablespoons Shaoxing rice wine, 1 teaspoon sugar, and 1 teaspoon sesame oil until the sugar dissolves. Set aside within easy reach of the stove.
Heat Wok (1 min)
Heat wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking—you should see wisps of smoke rising from the surface. Add 2 tablespoons vegetable oil and swirl to coat the bottom and sides of the wok.
Cook Fish (3 min)
Add fish pieces in a single layer, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding. Don't move the pieces for 1-2 minutes until they develop a golden crust on the bottom. Gently flip and cook another minute until just cooked through. The fish should be golden and firm but still tender. Transfer to a clean plate.
Stir-Fry Aromatics (1 min)
Add remaining 1 tablespoon vegetable oil to the wok. Add julienned ginger and scallion whites, stir-frying for 30 seconds until fragrant and the ginger begins to sizzle—be careful not to let them burn as they can turn bitter quickly.
Add Vegetables (2 min)
Add minced garlic, sliced bell pepper, and snow peas to the wok. Stir-fry constantly for 2 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender and bright in color. The snow peas should still have a slight crunch and the bell pepper should be just softened.
Finish and Combine (1 min)
Return the cooked fish to the wok along with the prepared sauce and scallion greens. Toss everything together gently for 30 seconds until the fish is heated through and everything is coated with the glossy sauce. Be gentle to avoid breaking the fish pieces.
Serve Immediately (1 min)
Transfer immediately to a serving plate and serve over steamed jasmine rice. The dish is best enjoyed right away while the vegetables retain their crisp texture and the fish is at its most tender. The abundant Shaoxing wine creates a light, flavorful sauce that perfectly complements steamed rice.
